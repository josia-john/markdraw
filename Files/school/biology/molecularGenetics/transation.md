# Processes
1:
The ribosome migrates along the mRNA (direction 5' 3'; logical especially in prokaryotes, (since translation happens during transcription) until it hits a start codon (AUG).

2:
A loaded tRNA with the anticodon matching the start codon (and thus a methionine as amino acid) attaches to the start codon in the ribosome. It occupies the P site of the ribosome.

3:
The 2nd binding site in the ribosome, the A site is also occupied by a matching, loaded tRNA.

4:
The two amino acids of the attached tRNAs are linked. A peptide bond is produced with the expenditure of energy.

5:
The ribosome now slides one triplet further towards 3'.

6:
The tRNA of the P site thus slips out of the ribosome. It detaches from the mRNA and its amino acid. In the cytosol, it will be loaded again with an amino acid.

7:
The A-site tRNA has thus slipped onto the P-site, leaving the A-site with the next codon free for the next matching, loaded tRNA.

8:
steps 3-7 are now repeated until the ribosome encounters a stop codon. Then the ribosome breaks down into its two subunits and the protein is released.
