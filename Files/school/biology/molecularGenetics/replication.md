# Model for Replication of DNA
## Conservative model
Just copy the dna
## Semiconservative model
copy the two strangs of dna and combine old + new
## Dispersive model
copy part by part

## Experiment
* Let dna be built with heavy N-molecules
* move them to light N-molecules

-&gt; centrifuge the results
Few half heavy dna, many light dna -&gt; semiconservative model

# Process of Replication
## DNA polymer*ase* (Enzyme)
Puts complementary nucleotides together to form new DNA
"Proof-Reading"
## Primer
Tells the polymerase where to start, starts with RNA strand
They get replaced by polymerase later
## RNA prim*ase* (Enzyme)
Makes the primers
## matrix / template
Original that is being copied
## helic*ase* (Enzyme)
Breaks h-bonding of the 2 strands (unzip)
## DNA lig*ase* (Enzyme)
Closes gaps between Okazaki fragment, forms bonds between them.
## Okazaki fragment
Lots of new DNA
## leading strand
Easily built, "right" direction
## lagging strand
"wrong" direction, can only attatch to 3'
## replication fork
Where the DNA is not yet complete
## origin
primers attached to starting points.
## Topoisomer*ase* (Enzyme)
unwinds the DNA structure, makes it easier for Helicase to open DNA.

## Description of Image
1: Helicase for unzipping
2: special proteins, prevent immediate reconnection
3: Polymerase for leading strand (III)
4: Primase for creating primers
5: Polymerase for lagging strand (III)
6: Polymerase for removing primers (I)
7: Ligase for closing gaps

# Errors during replication
Roughly 1 in 10^5 nucloetides is attached wrong.
But:
In the finished DNA there is only 1 in 10^9 wrong nucleotide

The Polymerase always checks if the connected nucleotide is the right one. If not, it corrects it's mistake.
