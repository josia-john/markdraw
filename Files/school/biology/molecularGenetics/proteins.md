# Central Dogma of Molecular Biology
We have: DNA, RNA, Protein

the flow of information looks as follows:
* DNA *transcription* ➔ RNA
* RNA *reverse transcription* ➔ DNA
* RNA *translation* ➔ Protein
* DNA *replication* ➔ DNA
* RNA *replication* ➔ RNA
* **NOT EXISTENT (current state):** Protein ➔ RNA

# From Gene to Protein
DNA ➔ RNA in nucleus
RNA ➔ ribosome in cytosol
ribosome ➔ protein in cytosol

# Transcription
Basically replication to build mRNA. RNA polymerase forms the RNA.
➔ has to find start and end point.

From one starting point, one can build 4 different mRNAs. You copy from that position on top / on bottom in one direction (2x2).
➔ *Take the complementary thing and pay attention to 3' and 5'*

mRNA polymerase only reads form 3' to 5' (building from 5' to 3'). Rules two out.
Promotor that tells the enzyme which strand it has to take.


# Example of mRNA
5' UUAUGGGCUACGCGGGUAACGAUUAUAUGUAA 3'

Read DNA strand is the antisense strand. The not read DNA strand looks like (T ➔ U) the RNA built.

1.The DNA is unwound by ahelicase.
2.The RNA polymerase **(.2.)** docks at a so-called promoter **(.1.)** section. The promoter indicates the start siteand the reading direction.Because the RNA polymerase can only read from 3' to 5'or can only join from 5' to 3', the given reading direction determineswhich of the two DNA single strands serves as the templateand therefore is being read.This strand is called the templatestrand(ornon-coding strand/ antisensestrand)**(.5.)** The other strand is called the coding strand(or sense strand) **(.6.)**
3.The RNA polymerase now migrates along the sensestrand and forms the corresponding complementary RNA **(.4.)** using RNA nucleotides **(.3.)**.The process proceeds as in replication, but since an RNA is formed, a U(racil)is incorporated as the complementary base to A of the sensestrand.
4.The freshly formed part of the mRNA detaches from the DNA during transcription and the DNA then closes again to form the double strand.
5.If the RNA polymerase reaches a certainpoint in the DNA, a so-called terminator **(.7.)** sequence, it terminates the transcription and the RNA is free.
