# Humidity
## States of Water
Solid: Ice
Liquid: Water
Gaseous: Vapor

## Transitions
Liquid ➔ Solid: Freeze
Solid ➔ Liquid: Melt
Liquid ➔ Gaseaus: Evaporate
Gaseaus ➔ Liquid :Condensate
Solid ➔ Gaseaus: Sublimate
Gaseaus ➔ Solid: Deposite (Resublimate)

## Units:
(what we have) **Absolute Humidity (AH)**: (two litres, 230 m^3) gramms / m^3 = 8.65 g/m^3
(what is max) **Saturation specific humidity (SAT)**: 17.3 g/m^3
(what we have, relative) **Relative Humidity (RH)** = Absolute Humidity / Saturation specific humidity * 100% = 50%

➔ Saturation Curve / dew point curve
Shows maximum amount of water in air (Saturation specific humidity) at given temperature.

# Percipitation
**convective percipitation**
1. Solar radiation heats up the ground + adjacent air.
➔ water evaporates

2. Heated air expands and starts rising (with the water vapor).
➔ convection currents

3. As air rises it cools down.

4. The water condensates out of the air. ➔ waterdroplets that form clouds.

5. Water droplets merge and then fall down as rain.


Condensation level is where the air is fully saturated, above that clouds form.

## Percipitation types
All start frozen, all except snow melt in air.

Rain: liquid droplet, different categories according to intensity (fog)

(Freezing Rain: Liquid droplets that freeze to ground)

Hail / Sleet: Freezes in air during fall
➔ Hail: Frozen Percipitation, Frozen Raindrops (&gt;5mm)
➔ ➔ Refreezes in the atmosphere
➔ Sleet (&lt;5mm)

Snow: No remelting. Mostly resublimation (Vapor ➔ Ice).
